amendment must never however contradict the principles embodied in this Constitution but solely relate to modifications of particular provisions which do not alter the spirit of the Constitution and such amendment requires that two thirds of the Storting agree thereto An amendment to the Constitution adopted in the manner aforesaid shall be signed by the President and the Secretary of the
